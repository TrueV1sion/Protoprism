Trial Evidence Is Real — But Time-Horizon Mismatched

SELECT and FLOW provide the strongest GLP-1 cost-offset evidence to date. Both trials demonstrate meaningful clinical benefits — over 34–39 month follow-up periods that structurally cannot be captured in a single 2026 plan year.

SELECT Trial: 20% MACE Reduction (HR 0.80)
Semaglutide 2.4mg reduced major adverse cardiovascular events by 20% (95% CI 0.72–0.90) in non-diabetic obese adults with established CVD. Mean follow-up: 39.8 months. NNT ~67 over 3 years — material hospitalization avoidance, but NOT fully captured in a single 2026 plan year. Applies only to ~15–20% of GLP-1 users with established CVD.
High — PRIMARY (NEJM 2023)
FLOW Trial: 24% Kidney Composite Reduction (HR 0.76)
Semaglutide 1.0mg reduced composite kidney outcomes in T2D patients with CKD. Creates a distinct, high-value cost offset category: dialysis avoidance at $90,000–$100,000/year per incident ESRD patient. Most defensible GLP-1 coverage case for MA payers with diabetic CKD populations — requires Ozempic, separate coverage pathway from obesity indication.
High — PRIMARY (NEJM 2024)
Tirzepatide Evidence Asymmetry
SURPASS-CVOT (NEJM Dec 2025) now provides T2D cardiovascular data for tirzepatide. However, the obesity-without-diabetes population — the primary growth driver — remains unproven. SURMOUNT-MMO results expected October 2027. Payers extrapolating semaglutide evidence to Zepbound users in general obesity are operating in an evidence vacuum.
High — CONVERGENT finding
RWE Claims Data: 10–20% Lower Total Medical Costs at 12 Months
Multiple retrospective cohort studies (IBM MarketScan, Optum, IQVIA) find GLP-1 users have 10–20% lower total medical costs vs. matched diabetic controls within 12 months — but offset magnitude increases substantially at 24–36 months. Critical for 2026: patients initiated in 2025–2026 generate primarily drug costs in Year 1, with offsets materializing in 2027–2028.
Medium — SECONDARY (Multiple RWE)
Offset Stratification: 3–4x Differential by Risk Tier
Hospitalization cost offsets are NOT uniform across GLP-1 users:
• High-CVD-risk (SELECT-eligible): $180–280 PMPM
• General obesity (no CVD): $40–80 PMPM
Drug cost: ~$900–$1,300 PMPM at list price. Even high-CVD offsets fall short of drug cost at 2026 realization rates.
Medium — Clinical RWE + Trial Data

Obesity-Only Evidence Vacuum

The fastest-growing GLP-1 segment (obesity without diabetes/CVD) has the weakest cost-offset evidence. SELECT enrolled patients with ESTABLISHED CVD — inapplicable to ~80% of commercial GLP-1 users. This critical gap persists until SURMOUNT-MMO (2027).
