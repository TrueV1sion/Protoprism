Cost Driver Analysis Dimension

Cost Driver Analysis: Deep-dive into the underlying factors driving Medicare spending changes - utilization, pricing, demographics, disease burden

1 agent analyzed this dimension: Health Economics Researcher.

Specialty drug spending has become increasingly concentrated in Medicare Part D, with a small percentage of beneficiaries driving disproportionate cost growth over the past decade.
Journal of Managed Care & Specialty Pharmacy (2024) analysis shows concentration patterns in Medicare Part D specialty drug spending from 2014-2024, with specialty medications representing the fastest-growing cost component of Part D spending.
Source: PubMed PMID: 39612254 - 'Concentration of spending and share of specialty drug spending in Medicare Part D over a 10-year period'
HIGH — PubMed PMID: 39612254 - 'Concentration of spending and share of specialty drug spending in Medicare Part D over a 10-year period'
Medicare Part D coverage gap closure differentially affects drug utilization, increasing branded drug use while generic utilization remains more stable.
Health Economics study (2023) demonstrated that coverage gap reforms led to increased utilization of branded medications while generic drug use showed less dramatic changes, indicating persistent price sensitivity in utilization decisions.
Source: PubMed PMID: 36399360 - 'Effects of Medicare Part D coverage gap closure on utilization of branded and generic drugs'
HIGH — PubMed PMID: 36399360 - 'Effects of Medicare Part D coverage gap closure on utilization of branded and generic drugs'
Regional demographic aging creates significant variation in Medicare-eligible population growth, with Sun Belt states experiencing the most rapid transitions.
2022 Census ACS data shows major state-level variations in 65+ populations: Florida (3.76 million), California (4.07 million), and Arizona (1.13 million), representing different demographic transition speeds and Medicare enrollment pressures.
Source: Census ACS 2022 - Age distribution by state (variables B01001_020E through B01001_049E)

Policy Impact Assessment Dimension

Policy Impact Assessment: Analysis of how regulatory changes, payment reforms, and policy interventions have affected Medicare spending trajectories

1 agent analyzed this dimension: Medicare Policy Analyst.

CMS is implementing two major drug pricing models (GUARD and GLOBE) in 2025-2026 that will fundamentally alter Medicare Part B and Part D inflation rebate calculations.
Federal Register documents 2025-23705 (GUARD Model) and 2025-23702 (GLOBE Model) published December 23, 2025, propose alternative payment methods for calculating inflation rebates for Part D and Part B drugs respectively. These models test whether changing rebate calculations reduces Medicare program costs while preserving quality of care.
Source: Federal Register documents 2025-23705 and 2025-23702, CMS proposed rules
HIGH — Federal Register documents 2025-23705 and 2025-23702, CMS proposed rules
CMS has imposed a 6-month nationwide moratorium on DMEPOS supplier enrollment, signaling intensified fraud prevention efforts that could affect Medicare spending patterns.
Federal Register Notice 2026-03971 published February 27, 2026, announces a nationwide temporary moratorium on Medicare enrollment of DMEPOS (Durable Medical Equipment, Prosthetics, Orthotics, and Supplies) supplier medical supply companies.
Source: Federal Register document 2026-03971, CMS Notice
HIGH — Federal Register document 2026-03971, CMS Notice
CMS is developing comprehensive anti-fraud regulations (CRUSH initiative) that could significantly impact Medicare spending through enhanced oversight and enforcement.
Federal Register document 2026-03968 published February 27, 2026, requests stakeholder feedback on potential regulatory changes for a 'Comprehensive Regulations To Uncover Suspicious Healthcare (CRUSH)' proposed rule to make CMS more effective in preventing fraud.
Source: Federal Register document 2026-03968, CMS RFI
