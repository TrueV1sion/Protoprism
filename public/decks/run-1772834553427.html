True Net Cost Exposure: WAC Is Not the Story

List prices anchor gross-to-net calculations, but actual payer cost exposure depends on indication, channel, and rebate leverage. The 2× diabetes/obesity differential is the critical cost driver.

WAC Range 2024–2025: $900–$1,350/Month
Wegovy (semaglutide OB) WAC ~$1,349/mo. Zepbound (tirzepatide) ~$1,060/mo. Ozempic (semaglutide DM) ~$935/mo. Net-after-rebate: $270–$800/mo depending on channel and indication.
High — Manufacturer Disclosures / CMS
Obesity Rebates ~Half of Diabetes Rebates
Commercial diabetes GLP-1s: 40–55% off WAC. Obesity indications: 20–35% off WAC (limited formulary competition). Effective net: ~$1,012/mo for obesity vs. ~$500/mo for diabetes — a 2× differential that directly drives payer coverage hesitancy.
Medium — IQVIA / Secondary Analysis
IRA Catastrophic Tier: 4× Per-Beneficiary Plan Liability
Pre-IRA plan sponsor liability in catastrophic phase: 15%. Post-IRA (2025): 60%. A Medicare beneficiary on Wegovy now generates $7,200–$9,600/year in plan sponsor catastrophic liability vs. $1,800–$2,400 pre-IRA.
