Tension Point

Clinical Evidence vs Financial Reality

Strong clinical outcomes conflict with cost-effectiveness thresholds requiring 81.9% price reduction

Clinical Justification

Evidence Supports Broad Coverage
15-20% weight loss with 20% MACE reduction in SELECT trial creates compelling clinical case for coverage across diabetes and obesity indications. CV benefits justify expanded population access.
  • STEP-1 and SURMOUNT-1 trial efficacy
  • Cardiovascular outcomes data
  • Quality of life improvements
  • Proven weight maintenance

Financial Constraints

Dramatic Price Reduction Required
Cost-effectiveness analysis indicates semaglutide needs 81.9% price reduction and tirzepatide needs 30.5% reduction to meet standard thresholds. Current pricing creates unsustainable budget impact.
  • $12,000-16,000 annual per-patient cost
  • 15-25% pharmacy spend increase
  • MLR threshold pressure
  • Cash flow timing mismatches
Resolution: Values-Based Stakeholder Strategy
This tension cannot be resolved through evidence alone—it reflects different stakeholder priorities. Clinical teams prioritize patient outcomes while finance teams prioritize sustainability. Recent manufacturer price cuts ($299-449/month) partially address the gap but fundamental tension remains. Strategy requires presenting both perspectives to leadership while developing scenario-based approaches for different pricing environments.

Tension Point

Coverage Generosity vs Budget Sustainability

Payers face competing pressures between using GLP-1 coverage for differentiation versus implementing restrictions

Competitive Differentiation

Generous Coverage Attracts Members
Plans offering broad GLP-1 obesity coverage gain competitive advantage in employer market and member acquisition. Copay caps become primary differentiation strategy.
  • Member acquisition advantage
  • Employer satisfaction benefits
  • Market share protection
  • Premium positioning opportunity

Budget Sustainability

States and Plans Eliminate Coverage
Budget impact forces coverage eliminations across Medicaid (13 vs 16 states) and commercial plans. Even generous coverage triggers 300-500% utilization spikes.
  • State Medicaid eliminations
  • Commercial plan restrictions
  • Utilization surge risk
  • Adverse selection exposure
Resolution: Scenario-Based Strategic Planning
Construct three scenarios: (1) Broad coverage becomes table stakes, (2) Coverage restrictions become industry norm, (3) Segmented market with different strategies. Develop strategies robust across scenarios: invest in adherence programs, negotiate innovative risk-sharing contracts, prepare infrastructure for both expansion and restriction. The winning approach will be adaptability rather than betting on a single outcome.
