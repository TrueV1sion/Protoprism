GLP-1 Pharmacy Trend: #1 Cost Driver 2025–2026
GLP-1-specific PMPM costs reaching $24–$27+ for employers covering obesity indications. Claims share of employer health costs rose from 6.9% to 10.5% in two years.
High — WTW, AssuredPartners, Evernorth (fact-checked)
Persistence Gap: 32–46% at 12 Months (Real-World)
Versus 80–90% in clinical trials. Source: Prime Therapeutics/JMCP 2024. Creates fundamental uncertainty in cost accumulation AND offset projections. A prior misattribution to IQVIA/AIM has been corrected.
High — Prime Therapeutics/JMCP 2024
No Biosimilar Competition Through 2026 Planning Horizon
Semaglutide compound patent expires December 2031; additional patents extend to ~2040. Payers have zero competitive supply-side pricing leverage. No compounding pharmacy alternative remains viable post-FDA shortage list removal (May 2025).
High — I-MAK, DrugPatentWatch (primary)
IRA Part D Catastrophic Phase: Structural Non-Discretionary Headwind
Plan liability in catastrophic phase increased from 15–20% to 60% effective 2025. Medicare Advantage plans covering T2D GLP-1s (Ozempic, Mounjaro) cannot restrict this coverage. This is locked-in regulatory risk independent of any payer strategy.
High — CMS primary source confirmed
MLR Deterioration Is Multi-Factorial — GLP-1 Not Sole Driver
CVS/Aetna 95.2%, Humana 89.9%, Elevance 89.5% MLR (Oliver Wyman Q3 2024). V28 risk model changes, Star Ratings impacts, Medicaid redeterminations, and utilization trends are co-equal headwinds. No payer publicly disaggregates GLP-1-specific MLR contribution. All bps estimates herein are MODELED PROJECTIONS.
High — Primary payer disclosures (fact-checker confirmed)
Medical Offsets: Real but Delayed 2–5 Years
SELECT trial (NEJM 2023) confirms 20% MACE reduction. Translates to $500–$1,500 PMPY offsets at sustained adherence. Zero of these offsets materialize in 2026 for members who enrolled in 2024–2025. Timing mismatch is the core MLR problem.
Medium — SELECT trial (primary); HEOR models (secondary)
