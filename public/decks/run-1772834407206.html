What We Know With Certainty

Ten fact-checked ground truths that all agents and the PRISM critic agree upon — the evidentiary bedrock for all subsequent analysis.

Drug Costs Are Immediate & Quantifiable

SELECT Trial: Only RCT-Grade CV Evidence in Obesity
Semaglutide 2.4mg, HR 0.80 (95% CI 0.72–0.90), N=17,604, 39.8 months mean follow-up, established CVD without diabetes. This is the SOLE RCT cardiovascular outcomes evidence for GLP-1 in an obesity population.
High — Primary NEJM
No Generic Semaglutide in 2026
Core patent expires 2031–2032; secondary/device patents extend to 2033+. Potential true generic entry ~2038. FDA compounding restrictions enforced April/May 2025 — courts upheld.
High — Fact-checker confirmed
Persistence Improving But Still Sub-Trial
1-year persistence: 32.3% (2021 cohorts) → 62.7% (2024 cohorts, Prime Therapeutics 2025). Materially reduces realized offsets vs. RCT projections. Still below clinical trial retention (~80%+).
High — Prime Therapeutics 2025
Tirzepatide Clinically Superior in Head-to-Head
SURMOUNT-5 (NEJM May 2025): 20.2% vs 13.7% body weight loss vs. semaglutide at 72 weeks (p<0.001), open-label RCT. SURMOUNT-MMO cardiovascular outcomes trial not reporting until ~2027.
High — NEJM confirmed
Prime Therapeutics: No Year 1–2 Medical Cost Reduction
The single strongest real-world ground-truth: NO statistically significant medical cost reduction in years 1–2 of GLP-1 use. This directly validates the structural pessimism for 2026 MLR modeling.
High — Confirmed ground truth

Confirmed selected for IPAY 2027 negotiation. Trump administration separate deal at $245/mo. Tirzepatide NOT selected for IPAY 2026 or 2027. MFP effective January 1, 2027.

Orforglipron NDA Dec 2025, FDA Action Early-Mid 2026
ATTAIN-1: 12.4% body weight reduction at 72 weeks (corrected from agent's erroneous 15% at 36 weeks). First oral non-peptide GLP-1. Approval timing uncertain — may slip to late 2026 or 2027.

Compounding Crackdown: Upside Risk to MLR
FDA enforcement redirects patients from $150–300/mo compounded to $1,000+ WAC branded products. Estimated 500K–1M users affected†. Directionally cost-increasing; magnitude uncertain due to insured-vs-cash-pay split gap.
Medium — Primary FDA enforcement data
Pipeline Expansion: 4–6 Branded Options by 2026
Beyond semaglutide/tirzepatide: orforglipron (oral, NDA Dec 2025), CagriSema (Phase 3), survodutide (MASH+obesity), retatrutide (Phase 3). Each creates indication-specific pricing segments with different MLR calculus.
High — Primary trial registries
Tirzepatide: Clinically Better AND Cheaper Than Wegovy
Tirzepatide WAC ~$290/month below Wegovy WAC. Superior efficacy (SURMOUNT-5). This unusual alignment — clinical guidelines and cost management pointing same direction — creates rare formulary consensus. But CV outcomes evidence is absent until ~2027.
High — SURMOUNT-5 NEJM confirmed
Formulary Wars Era Beginning
With 3–4 branded GLP-1 options competing in 2026, exclusive formulary deals become financially viable. PBM rebate leverage increases significantly. GLP-1 formulary exclusions (covering only one molecule) will drive premium rebate negotiations — fundamentally changing the commercial pricing dynamic.
Medium — Domain inference
Oral GLP-1 Could Expand Penetration 20–40%†
Orforglipron removes injection-barrier historically limiting GLP-1 penetration in lower-adherence populations. ACA non-discrimination provisions may limit payer ability to restrict oral coverage. Access expansion simultaneously dilutes population risk profile — worsening aggregate offset ratio.
Medium — Secondary inference
