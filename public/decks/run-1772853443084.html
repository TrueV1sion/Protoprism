REGULATORY Regulatory Environment Scanner

The Regulatory Demand Machine

Five concurrent CMS mandates are creating a compliance environment of unprecedented complexity. Each is an independent demand catalyst. Together they are something else entirely — see Slide 11.

CY2027 Stars Methodology Reset

Removes 12 measures (7 operational, 3 process, 2 patient experience), adds Depression Screening. Modeling: 89% of MA contracts see Star score declines; $1.3B QBP destruction.

▲ CRITICAL DEMAND DRIVER | Plans urgently need Stars scenario modeling & simulation

CMS-HCC V28 — 100% Implementation PY2026

Removes 2,000+ ICD-10 codes from HCC mapping. Expected 3–8% risk score declines for non-adapting plans. OIG escalating RADV audit intensity. V28 completion + unlinked chart review exclusion = compounding documentation pressure.

▲ CRITICAL DEMAND DRIVER | Prospective documentation accuracy tools required

CMS-0057-F Prior Authorization Mandate

Phase 1 (live Jan 1, 2026): 72-hour expedited, 7-day standard turnaround + public metrics reporting. Phase 2 (Jan 1, 2027): FHIR-based PA API requirements. $15B projected 10-year savings creates immediate spending wave. Enforcement certainty: CERTAIN for Phase 1, uncertain for Phase 2 under Trump deregulatory posture.

▲ CRITICAL — Phase 1 LIVE | Phase 2 enforcement under watch

NCQA HEDIS dQM Transition — 100% FHIR-ECDS by MY2030

Hybrid methodology retirement by MY2029. MY2026 migration already in progress (Lead Screening, etc.). Vendors without FHIR-native HEDIS certification lose status. NCQA Digital Content Services standardizes measure logic — shifts differentiation toward data connectivity and analytics. Inovalon's 25 consecutive certifications = strong position during transition.

⚠ HIGH COMPLEXITY | 4-year transition window; certification moat active now

TEFCA / Information Blocking Enforcement

TEFCA: 10M to 500M document exchanges in 14 months. ONC first letters of nonconformity Feb 2026. HTI-5 proposing FHIR-centricity, removing 34 certification criteria. Near-term: enriches Inovalon's dataset. Medium-term: standardizes access, eroding proprietary connectivity advantage.

⚠ TIME-DEPENDENT | Strengthens moat 2026–2028; erodes it 2029+

CY2027 MA Advance Notice: +0.09% Rate

Essentially flat payment vs. industry expectation of 4–6%. Unlinked chart review diagnoses excluded from risk scores starting 2027. Creates 'do more with less' dynamic — compresses plan budgets while compliance requirements intensify. Favors consolidated platform vendors over point solutions.

ⓘ MIXED | Revenue pressure + analytics urgency simultaneously

TENSIONS Strategic Complexity Map

Five Tensions That Cannot Be Flattened

These represent genuine strategic complexity preserved rather than artificially resolved. Each requires executive judgment. The resolutions below are scenario-based, not prescriptive.

⚠ MA Financial Stress: Demand Driver vs. Demand Suppressor
Side A (Regulatory + Domain): Plans facing $1.3B QBP destruction increase analytics spend to recover Stars ratings. Recovery urgency = highest willingness to pay.

Side B (Market): Severely distressed plans cut vendor budgets, consolidate, or exit markets entirely. Humana −400K members is a direct revenue risk.

Resolution: Both true simultaneously for different segments. See Emergence Slide 12 for three-segment model.
⚠ Platform Breadth: Enterprise Strength vs. Functional Weakness
Side A (Domain): Platform wins at CIO/CTO vendor rationalization. Multiplicative switching costs during concurrent compliance mandates.

Side B (Market): Reveleer ($300M+ risk adjustment), Cohere Health (PA), Cotiviti outperform in functional evaluations.

Resolution: Both correct at different buying levels. CY2027 upheaval temporarily strengthens enterprise argument — time-limited window.
⚠ CMS-0057-F: Phase 2 Enforcement Certainty
Side A (Regulatory): Phase 1 live and irreversible. Bipartisan Congressional support. AHIP not opposing core mandate.

Side B (Market): Trump deregulatory posture could delay Phase 2 to 2028–2029. Historical precedent of CMS mandate delays.

Resolution: Direction of travel irreversible. Calibrate to Phase 1 certainty; maintain Phase 2 optionality.
▲ Data Moat: Genuinely Defensible vs. Structurally Eroding
Side A (Domain): 397M patients / 86.6B events — continuously compounding. TEFCA enforcement near-term enriches dataset. 25 years of NCQA continuity is irreplicable.

Side B (Market + Regulatory): TEFCA scaling 10M → 500M exchanges in 14 months. HEDIS dQM standardizes formats. Optum 100M+ lives. AI democratizes analytics.

Resolution: Strong now; time-bounded. Moat peaks 2026–2028. See Emergence Slide 14.
▲ PE Margin Extraction vs. Growth Investment Requirements
Side A (Domain + Market): Nordic Capital requires exit-ready EBITDA expansion and recurring revenue growth for $10–15B valuation target.

Side B (Regulatory + Domain): PA automation (CMS-0057-F), HEDIS dQM retooling, AI capabilities, and provider market diversification all require sustained or increased investment.

Resolution: Values conflict requiring human judgment. IPO = growth narrative; strategic sale = margin profile. Tension intensifies as exit window opens.
