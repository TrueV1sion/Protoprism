Clinical Evidence Assessment

Real-World Clinical Performance

Evidence base for AI clinical efficacy and deployment outcomes

AI Diagnostic Imaging: Proven Efficacy
FDA has approved 100+ AI/ML-based medical devices as of 2024, with significant concentration in radiology. IDx-DR (diabetic retinopathy screening) showed 87.4% sensitivity and 90.7% specificity, establishing proof-of-concept for AI clinical efficacy.
High — Primary sources
AI Sepsis Prediction: Clinical Impact
Johns Hopkins TREWS system showed 18% reduction in sepsis mortality and 1.5-day reduction in LOS in NEJM 2022 study. Demonstrates clinical efficacy but reveals implementation science as critical bottleneck.
Medium — Primary sources
Mental Health AI: Evidence Gap
Digital therapeutics for depression (Woebot, Wysa) report user engagement rates of 60-80% but only 2 published RCTs with sample sizes >100. Significant evidence gap despite high commercial interest.
Medium — Secondary sources
Surgical AI: Cost-Effectiveness Controversy
Da Vinci surgical systems with AI guidance show 15-25% reduction in operative time but cost-effectiveness remains controversial with mixed payer coverage decisions, highlighting health economics as major adoption barrier.

Regulatory Framework Analysis

Regulatory Arbitrage Landscape

Federal deregulation meets state enforcement acceleration

FDA January 2026 Guidance - Deregulation

Removed premarket review requirements for many clinical decision support tools and expanded general wellness device exemptions, creating enforcement discretion for single-output CDS software.

MARKET EXPANSION | $15-25B total addressable market for wellness AI

State AI Oversight - Enforcement Acceleration

California AB 489, Texas TRAIGA, and multi-state initiatives require healthcare providers to disclose AI use, with professional licensing board enforcement authority beginning January 2026.

COMPLIANCE BURDEN | $50M+ annually for large platforms

CMS WISeR Model Launch

First Medicare program to use AI for prior authorization decisions on traditional Medicare services, launched January 1, 2026 in six states (NJ, OH, OK, TX, AZ, WA).

PRECEDENT SET | $2-4B market for AI utilization management
HIPAA Security Rule Faces AI-Specific Update
HHS OCR proposed first major HIPAA Security Rule update in 20 years (December 2024), citing AI risks. Healthcare AI projects show 66% fail initial HIPAA BAA audits, with training data memorization creating new compliance vulnerabilities requiring $5-15M in redesign costs.
